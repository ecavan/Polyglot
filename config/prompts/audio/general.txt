Prime for conversational North American English, including non-native accents, crosstalk, and shifting subjects. Terms you will hear, spelled correctly: podcast, episode, livestream, subscriber, follow-up, deadline, schedule, budget, meeting, agenda, feedback, update, link, app, website, email, online, okay, yeah, gonna, wanna, kinda. Translating to Québécois French: keep the anglicisms QC speakers actually use in speech (le podcast, le link, le feedback, c'est cool, le fun, OK, le budget, le meeting or la réunion; email or courriel both natural). Use real French where Montrealers normally do: schedule = cédule or horaire, deadline = échéance, update = mise à jour, subscriber = abonné, follow-up = suivi, website = site web, livestream = diffusion en direct. Avoid France-isms (no "planning"). Keep proper nouns, brand names, hashtags, handles, numbers, dates, prices, percentages, and units verbatim.

Diarization: roles vary, so label generically and consistently (Host, Guest, Caller, or Speaker 1/2) per distinct voice. Pitfalls: rapid casual speech, fillers and false starts, overlapping turns, varied accents, music, laughter, background noise, mumbled brand names, and spelled-out acronyms. If a word is genuinely unintelligible, omit it rather than guess.
